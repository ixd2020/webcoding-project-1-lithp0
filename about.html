Timeline of Oscar-Claude Monet

1840

Monet was born.

1845

Monet and his family move to Le Harve, Normandy. There, his interest in art is piqued and he learns to paint nature.

1856

Monet's meets Eugene Boudin, who taught him en plien air (outdoor) techniques for painting.

1857

Monet's mother, who had encouraged his love for painting, passes away.

1862

Monet is disillusioned by traditional art taught in art school and becomes a student of Charles Gleyre in Paris, along with Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Frederic Basille and Alfred Sisley. Together they popularised Impressionism.

1867

Monet's first son is born.

1870

The Franco-Prussian war begins, and Monet and his family seek refuge in England a year later he moves to the Netherlands where he painted 25 paintings. Monet also gets married to Camille Monet.

1874

The first Impressionist exhibition is held in Paris. Monet and Renoir take part.

a few of his paintings that were on display

1876

Camille Monet contracts Tubercolosis. Their second son is born two years later, further weakening her health.

1879

Camille Monet passes away, and Claude makes studies of oil of his deceased wife. After several difficult months of grief, he creates some of his best pieces of the 19th century. His best friend's wife, Alice Hoschede, raises his children alongside her own in Paris

some of his paintings during this time

1883

After moving multiple times, he and Alice settle down in Giverny, where he planted a garden and painted for much of the rest of his life.

Monet's garden in 1989

1892

Following the death of her estranged husband, Monet and Alice marry.

1893

Monet undertakes an extensive landscaping project to grow water lilies, which had to be then imported from Egypt and South America, as well as a Japanese style bridge over the lake. They are the subject of his paintings over the course of the next 20 years.

Paintings of Monet's garden

1911

Alice passes away, and three years later, so does his eldest son. He begins to develop cataracts.

1923

Monet undergoes cataract surgery, and his paintings begin to take a reddish hue. He repaints some of his waterlilies, and is said to have been able to see ultraviolet wavelengths; hence making his water lilies a deeper shade of blue.

Examples of bluer and redder artworks done post surgery.

1926

Monet passes away from lung cancer.
