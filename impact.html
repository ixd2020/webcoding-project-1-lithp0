Impact of Monet's work

Claude Monet is considered one of the driving forces behind impressionism. His focus on capturing light and objects as how they were perceived by the human eye rather than the subject itself became a staple of Impressionism, popularised by himself and his artist friends. He studied the effects of the weather and time of day by painting the same subject repeatedly, such as the waterlillies series. in different lights, weather and seasons. Considered a father of modern art by rejecting what was traditionally tauught, his works went on to inspire Paul Cezzane, and gained fame only after his death while living most of his life in poverty.
